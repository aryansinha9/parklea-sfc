Registration Information

Player Registrations are now open

ALL Registrations must be completed online via Play Football.

Register on Play Football

Claiming Your Account as an Existing Participant

If you're already registered with Play Football, ensure the details you enter during registration (First Name, Last Name, Date of Birth) exactly match your existing account. If there's a mismatch, please contact your Play Football Support team for assistance before proceeding. This helps prevent duplicate accounts and ensures your registration is accurate and eligibility is maintained.

Step-by-step guides are provided below for ease of participants claiming their account in the following relevant circumstances:

  • As an existing participant claiming their account during the registration flow
  • As an existing participant claiming their account through your participant profile
  • As someone registering a participant on behalf of somebody else

New Participants

If you do not have a Play Football account, you will click on the Create Account button and follow the instructions.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does the season begin?
The season will begin in early April (TBC) and end in mid-August for all Under 5 to Under 11 teams and competition teams that do not qualify for the finals. Depending on their progress, the teams that qualify for the finals will play an additional one to three weeks.
Where is the club located?
We are based at Morgan Power Reserve on Vardys Road in Kings Langley.
How do I register?
Registrations can only be completed online using PlayFootball. You can access registrations by visiting our website and clicking the quick link.
Can I use my Active Kids Vouchers when registering?
Yes, we are an approved Active Kids Provider. Please ensure you claim your voucher before you start the registration process, as it needs to be applied when you register.
What age groups are available?
We have teams for boys and girls starting at Under 5, all the way up to our over 30s Women's and Over 35's Men's teams. The age group is based on the age that the player turns in 2026.
Do you conduct working with children checks?
All members of our club who deal with children under 18 must have a valid Working with Children Check/Number. This includes Committee Members, Coaches and Managers.
Which team will my child be in?
The team your child plays in will be determined based on their ability. The decision will be made after our grading sessions.
What is grading?
Grading is a process used to determine the best fit for the player to ensure they are playing in the correct division based on their abilities. This will help ensure that each player has the best experience possible. At Parklea SFC, grading occurs for teams under 8 and up.
When is grading held?
Grading sessions will be held in early to mid-February. Times and locations will be published soon on Facebook and our website. All players must be registered before attending grading. If you cannot participate in a grading session, please inform the club by emailing the committee at parklea@bdsfa.com.
Where are games played?
For Under 8s and up, games are played against other clubs within the BDSFA (Blacktown District Football Soccer Association). Approximately half the games will be at our home ground, Morgan Power Reserve and the other half will be away across different fields in the Blacktown District.

For our Under 5s, 6s, and 7s, Parklea SFC runs an In-house experience. All of these games are held at Morgan Power Reserve. There are two time slots: one for the Under 5s and 6s and one for the Under 7s. These times will be the same throughout the whole season.
When is training on and where?
Training for all teams is always at the same location, Morgan Power Reserve. Days are as follows for each age group:

Under 5s & 6s - TBC
Under 7s - TBC
Under 8 & 9s - TBC
Under 10s & 11s - TBC
Under 12s and up: These will differ for each team based on the coach’s decision. Once you are placed on a team, the coach or manager will advise you when training will be held. A training schedule will be released in early March.
How will we know where the games are each week?
Before the season starts, a draw will be published by the BDSFA, which shows all your game times and locations for the year. This will be published on DRIBL and is subject to change for reasons which may include but are not limited to field closures due to weather, re-grading, etc. Please check your DRIBL app before each game to ensure no changes have been made.
What days are games held?
Friday Nights: Over 30s Women, Over 35s Men
Saturdays: U5s to U16s Boys/Mixed, U18s Boys, Men’s Premier League
Sundays: Under 9 to U21 Girls, U21s Boys, All Age Men, All Age Women, Women’s Premier League
Are games played during the school holidays?
There are only two weekends without games: Easter weekend and the June long weekend. However, if any previous weekends have been rained out, the June long weekend may be used for catch-up games.
Will I need to buy my club kit?
The club will provide all teams from U5s to U21s with playing jerseys, and you will need to return them at the end of the season. Shorts and socks can be purchased from the club. Club shorts and socks MUST be worn on game days. Players must also have their own football boots and shin pads. All NEW Under 5s, 6s & 7s players receive a pair of socks and shorts in their registration fee.

Important Note

Football is a team sport! When you register, you are expected to be able to attend all games and training sessions.

If you cannot make a training session or game, you must notify your coach and manager with reasonable notice. Continued missed games and training sessions without a reasonable excuse may result in de-registration.
